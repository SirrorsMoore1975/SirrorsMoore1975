# Keep Android Open

## Introduction

`Google` in August 2025 has announced by September 2026, all android
applications must registered by a verified developers with `Google`
in order for it to be installed in any android device. They called this
movement is to make android environment `safer`. But applications such as
`F-Droid` applications store are unlikely to be able to comply. Many has
suspected Google main target is to block all side-loading applications, gain
control to what can and cannot install inside your android device, reduce use of custom ROMs. 

### Why is the move striping down freedom

Instead of you are in control of your own physical Android phone, 
`Google` become the judge and gatekeeper of your phone and decide
 what can or cannot be installed inside your phone. Sideloading
 application, emulator, customized ROM will become impossible.  
  
Termux, a powerful Linux terminal emulator on Android, was banned by Goggle due to `policy` concern. You can only use other store such as F-Droid, official github release, or download the apk file to install it.

> ... __all__ apps from non-registered developers __will be blocked__ once their lock-down goes into effect.

### What can you do

### DO

- Support them by using their free and open communities applications more; 
- Donating, buy those affected a coffee, let them know you are supporting them;
- Referencing them in social media (such as X formerly "Twitter"); - Associate to those applications; 
- Draw your friends or others attention how this will impact them;
- Feedback your privacy concern to `Google` channel whenever possible.

### DO NOT

- buy in to the requirement of registering as a registered developer.

They are fruiting from the benefit of the FOSS and grow into something like `Apple` or `Samsung` which monopoly and siding for company benefit. Android is and always has been free and open. The deadline marked the death of users privacy and security while using Android. They are using their position to make you to comply so that you give up those rights.
